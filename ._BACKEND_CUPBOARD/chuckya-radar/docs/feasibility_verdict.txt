SHORT ANSWER
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
YES — CHUCKYA works as a personal radar for local, life-critical
detection (meters to low hundreds of meters) using wearables,
UWB anchors, and phone BLE.

NO — It cannot reliably or lawfully passively detect another
person's phone number or precise location at 1-3 km without
carrier cooperation or explicit consent.

DETECTION OPTIONS COMPARISON
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Method                  Range               Accuracy         Privacy Risk
  ─────────────────────── ─────────────────── ──────────────── ───────────
  UWB anchors + tags      50-200 m            10-30 cm indoor  Low
  BLE advertising / AoA   5-100 m optimistic  Meter-level      Medium
  Explicit GPS sharing    Global              High absolute    High (opt-in)
  Carrier location APIs   Kilometres          Variable         Very high
  Hybrid (all above)      Local to regional   Best coverage    Controlled

WHY THE LIMITS EXIST (PLAIN TERMS)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  BLE and UWB are short-range radio technologies.
  - UWB gives radar-like ranging but practical range is tens of
    meters to low hundreds in real environments.
  - BLE can detect presence but distance estimates are noisy.
  - Long range requires infrastructure or consent.
  - Detecting a non-consenting phone at kilometres requires either
    carrier location data or that the user explicitly shares GPS.
  - Passive scanning cannot legally or reliably reach 1-3 km.

PRACTICAL ARCHITECTURE LAYERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Layer 1: LOCAL (always-on, immediate)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Bracelet (UWB/BLE): panic button + UWB ranging + BLE advertising
  - Phone app: receives CBOR, signs, shows local warning, uploads
  - Edge hub (NUC): aggregates anchors, fast triangulation, AI scoring

  Layer 2: REGIONAL (situational awareness)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Consented GPS sharing: opt-in live GPS with trusted circle/police
  - Carrier integration: lawful limited queries via MOUs (high overhead)

  Layer 3: CLOUD (verification and audit)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Verifier checks signatures, writes immutable audit artifacts
  - Risk scoring and two-person export for PII

OPERATIONAL RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  [ ] Default privacy: civilians see only distance/direction
  [ ] No coordinates unless explicitly consented
  [ ] Two-person export: Ops + Legal approval required
  [ ] Anti-spoofing: nonces, TTLs, device attestation
  [ ] Rate limits: prevent mass false alarms and probing
  [ ] No passive mass surveillance
  [ ] No covert MAC-to-phone-number mapping
  [ ] Keys stay in device keystore; server key in Secret Manager
