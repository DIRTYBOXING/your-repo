 DATA FIGHT CENTRAL — TAKEDOWN & EVIDENCE SOP
 Date: 28 March 2026
 Classification: INTERNAL — Legal / Ops
============================================================

PURPOSE: Standard operating procedure for content takedowns,
DMCA notices, and evidence preservation during and after
live events.

============================================================
 1. EVIDENCE COLLECTION (do BEFORE sending takedown)
============================================================

CAPTURE IMMEDIATELY:
  [ ] Screenshot of infringing content with URL visible
  [ ] Timestamp (UTC) of discovery
  [ ] Platform/host where content appears
  [ ] Archive URL using web.archive.org or similar
  [ ] If video: screen-record 30+ seconds showing infringement

EXTRACT FROM DFC SYSTEMS:
  [ ] Forensic watermark ID from pirated content
  [ ] Map watermark ID -> userId, entitlementId, sessionId
  [ ] Playback token logs (jti, iat, exp, device fingerprint)
  [ ] Playback start/stop timestamps
  [ ] CDN access logs for the session
  [ ] Stripe purchase record for the entitlement

STORE:
  Location: ops-audit/piracy/<date>-<incident-id>/
  Files:
    - screenshot.png
    - screen-recording.mp4
    - watermark-trace.json
    - playback-logs.json
    - cdn-logs.txt
    - stripe-receipt.json
    - timeline.txt (chronological narrative)

============================================================
 2. DMCA TAKEDOWN NOTICE (template)
============================================================

Send to the hosting provider's designated DMCA agent.

---BEGIN NOTICE---

To: [Hosting provider DMCA agent email]
Subject: DMCA Takedown Notice — Data Fight Central

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing to notify you of copyright infringement on
your platform pursuant to the Digital Millennium Copyright
Act (17 U.S.C. Section 512).

IDENTIFICATION OF COPYRIGHTED WORK:
The copyrighted work is a live/recorded combat sports event
streamed exclusively on the Data Fight Central platform
(https://datafightcentral.web.app), owned by Data Fight
Central, ABN [INSERT ABN].

IDENTIFICATION OF INFRINGING MATERIAL:
The infringing material is located at:
  URL: [INSERT URL]
  Description: [Unauthorized restream/recording of our event]

GOOD FAITH STATEMENT:
I have a good faith belief that the use of the copyrighted
material described above is not authorized by the copyright
owner, its agent, or the law.

ACCURACY STATEMENT:
The information in this notification is accurate, and under
penalty of perjury, I am authorized to act on behalf of the
copyright owner.

CONTACT INFORMATION:
  Name      : [YOUR LEGAL NAME]
  Business  : Data Fight Central, ABN [INSERT ABN]
  Email     : admin@datafightcentral.com
  Address   : [INSERT ADDRESS]
  Phone     : [INSERT PHONE]

SIGNATURE:
  [YOUR NAME]
  Date: [DATE]

---END NOTICE---

============================================================
 3. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC TAKEDOWN STEPS
============================================================

YOUTUBE:
  1. Go to youtube.com/copyright_complaint_form
  2. Select "Copyright" -> "I found content that infringes
     my copyright"
  3. Fill form with URLs and evidence
  4. Submit

TWITCH:
  1. Email dmca@twitch.tv with DMCA notice
  2. Include stream URL and timestamps
  3. Follow up if not actioned within 24 hours

FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM:
  1. Use facebook.com/help/intellectual_property
  2. Select "Copyright" and fill form
  3. Attach evidence screenshots

TWITTER/X:
  1. Use help.twitter.com/forms/dmca
  2. Fill DMCA form with URLs and evidence

GENERIC HOSTING:
  1. Find hosting provider via WHOIS lookup
  2. Find DMCA agent contact (usually in Terms of Service)
  3. Send DMCA notice template above

============================================================
 4. AUSTRALIAN COPYRIGHT NOTICE (alternative to DMCA)
============================================================

For Australian-hosted content, use Australian copyright law
rather than DMCA:

---BEGIN NOTICE---

NOTICE OF COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT
Pursuant to the Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)

[Same structure as DMCA notice above but reference:]
  - Copyright Act 1968 (Cth), Section 36 (infringement)
  - Copyright Act 1968 (Cth), Section 115 (remedies)

[Include same evidence and contact information]

---END NOTICE---

============================================================
 5. INTERNAL RESPONSE TIMELINE
============================================================

  | Time         | Action                                   |
  |--------------|------------------------------------------|
  | 0-15 min     | Discover and capture evidence             |
  | 15-30 min    | Extract watermark and identify source     |
  | 30-60 min    | Revoke source entitlement, send takedown  |
  | 1-24 hours   | Follow up on takedown, prepare legal pack |
  | 24-72 hours  | Escalate if not actioned; consider legal  |
  | 72+ hours    | Engage counsel for legal action if needed |

============================================================
 6. LEGAL ESCALATION CRITERIA
============================================================

ESCALATE TO COUNSEL IF:
  - Mass piracy (>5 unauthorized restreams)
  - Takedown not actioned within 72 hours
  - Commercial piracy (selling access to our content)
  - Repeat offender (same person/entity)
  - International jurisdiction complications

EVIDENCE PACKAGE FOR COUNSEL:
  - All items from Section 1 (evidence collection)
  - Copies of takedown notices sent
  - Responses from hosting providers
  - Watermark trace proving source identification
  - Financial impact estimate
