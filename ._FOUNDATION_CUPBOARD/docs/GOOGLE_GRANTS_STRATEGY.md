# Google Grants Campaign Structure for DFC Foundation

## Overview

Google Grants provides $10,000/month in free Google Ads for eligible nonprofits. This document outlines the complete campaign structure for DFC Foundation campaigns in Australia and New Zealand.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

### ✅ DFC Foundation Must:

1. Be a registered charity/nonprofit in Australia or New Zealand
2. Have a functional, content-rich website
3. Acknowledge Google Grants on the website
4. Have no commercial pages or paid content on donation pages
5. Maintain a minimum 5% click-through rate (CTR)
6. Follow Google Ads policies

### 📋 Application Steps:

1. Register for Google for Nonprofits
2. Verify nonprofit status
3. Apply for Google Grants
4. Wait for approval (2-4 weeks)
5. Set up campaigns and tracking

## Keyword Strategy

### Keyword Match Types:

- **Exact Match**: [keyword] — Highest relevance, lowest volume
- **Phrase Match**: "keyword" — Moderate relevance and volume
- **Broad Match Modifier**: +keyword +phrase — Wider reach while maintaining relevance

### Quality Score Optimization:

- Target Quality Score of 7+ (out of 10)
- Ensure landing pages match ad copy and keywords
- Include keywords in ad headlines and descriptions
- Fast-loading landing pages (< 3 seconds)
- Mobile-friendly design

### Negative Keywords:

- jobs, salary, careers (avoid job seekers)
- free, download, torrent (avoid non-donors)
- news, government, statistics (informational intent only)

## Landing Page Requirements

### Essential Elements for All Pages:

1. **Clear Headline**: Match ad copy (e.g., "Help Underprivileged Kids")
2. **Compelling Stats**: Use Australia/NZ poverty and violence statistics
3. **Donation CTA**: Multiple clear "Donate Now" buttons
4. **Impact Transparency**: Show exactly where funds go
5. **Social Proof**: Real stories, testimonials, community verification
6. **Trust Signals**: Charity registration, tax deductibility, partner logos
7. **Mobile Optimization**: Fast load times, responsive design
8. **UTM Tracking**: Track conversions from Google Ads

### UTM Parameters Example:

```

```

## Conversion Tracking

### Goals to Track:

1. **Donation Completed**: User completes payment

- `donate_click` — User clicks donate button
- `donate_complete` — Donation successful
- `signup_complete` — Account created
- `subscribe_newsletter` — Email captured

## Performance Benchmarks

### Google Grants Requirements:

- **Minimum CTR**: 5% (across all campaigns)
- **Account Activity**: Log in at least once every 90 days
- **Ad Quality**: No single-word keywords (with exceptions for medical terms, etc.)
- **Geo-Targeting**: Must be set appropriately

### DFC Foundation Goals:

- **CTR**: Target 8-12% (above minimum)
- **Conversion Rate**: 2-5% (donation or signup)
- **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: $10-$20 per donor
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: 5:1 (for every $1 spent, $5 donated)

## Monthly Reporting

### Metrics to Track:

- Total impressions, clicks, CTR
- Donations received (count and amount)
- New signups (fighters, fans, gyms)
- Cost per donation
- Campaign-specific performance
- Landing page conversions
- Geographic performance (AU vs NZ)

### Optimization Actions:

- Pause underperforming keywords (CTR < 3%)
- Increase budget for high-performing campaigns
- A/B test ad copy monthly
- Update landing pages based on conversion data
- Add new negative keywords
- Expand winning keyword groups

## Compliance & Best Practices

### Google Grants Policy Compliance:

- No keywords bidding on brand names (unless it's your own)
- Landing pages must have substantial content (avoid thin pages)
- No "donate" buttons on commercial pages
- Website must have an SSL certificate (https://)
- Surveys and tracking must comply with privacy policies

### DFC-Specific Best Practices:

- Highlight combat sports community involvement
- Emphasize transparency and community verification
- Use real stories (with consent) in ads and landing pages
- Leverage the DFC Ninja branding (mystical guardian)
- Cross-promote campaigns (Gold Coin Drive ↔ Pink Shield)
