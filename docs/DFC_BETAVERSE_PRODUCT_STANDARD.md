# DFC Betaverse Product Standard

## Purpose

This standard translates the Betaverse doctrine into product requirements.

DFC should build digital and immersive experiences that use combat-sports energy to develop stronger people, stronger communities, and safer online behavior. The product should not reward poisoning, humiliation, addiction loops, or community decay.

## Product North Star

Create the healthiest combat-sports digital ecosystem possible by making safety, respect, mentorship, recovery, and collaboration default behaviors across feed, chat, community, event, and immersive surfaces.

## Non-Negotiable Standards

### 1. Healthy By Default

- safe defaults beat permissive defaults
- youth-sensitive protections must be built in from day one
- the platform must not algorithmically reward outrage, pile-ons, or degradation

### 2. Mentor-Led Design

- high-adrenaline content should lead into guidance, context, and learning
- AI and community flows should promote discipline and growth over compulsive use
- DFC should feel like a coach and operator, not a casino for attention

### 3. No Poison In The Pool

- harassment, defamation, exploitation, manipulation, and explicit contamination are product risks, not edge cases
- repeated toxic behavior must be contained quickly and visibly at the systems level

### 4. Collaboration First

- DFC should be able to integrate rivals, partners, promotions, and external tools when doing so strengthens the ecosystem
- openness to collaboration should appear in product surfaces, not just brand language

### 5. Real-World Benefit

- success is not just session time or impressions
- success means healthier communities, stronger partnerships, better conduct, and more useful real-world outcomes

## Core Product Areas

### Communications Standard

All communication surfaces should be structured to reduce chaos and reward signal.

Required behaviors:

- threaded, high-context discussion over noise-heavy sprawl
- strong report, mute, block, and escalation tools
- anti-dogpile protections in comments and replies
- friction on repeated harassment behavior
- verified-expert and mentor visibility where appropriate

### Feed Standard

The feed is a health surface, not just a content surface.

Required behaviors:

- down-rank rage bait, humiliation, gossip warfare, and social poisoning
- up-rank education, recovery, respectful analysis, community uplift, and verified event promotion
- prevent explicit-content contamination in general audience lanes
- support promotion amplification without encouraging abusive hype mechanics

### Community Standard

DFC communities should strengthen people instead of turning them against one another.

Required behaviors:

- mentorship and recognition mechanics
- reputation built on contribution quality, not intimidation
- support-driven community prompts and rituals
- clear anti-trolling, anti-defamation, anti-exploitation enforcement

### Metaverse / Immersive Standard

Immersive spaces must have a purpose.

Required behaviors:

- moderated rooms for learning, collaboration, event discovery, training visualization, partner engagement, and healthy social participation
- clear room types, room rules, and host controls
- no empty novelty worlds built only for vanity metrics
- youth-aware access controls and safety layers where needed

### AI Standard

AI should help restore human quality, not degrade it.

Required behaviors:

- calm, respectful language
- early detection of distress, harassment, escalation, and manipulation
- guidance toward healthier participation
- no shaming, baiting, or synthetic dependency tactics

### Youth Safety Standard

Youth safety should be product architecture, not policy wallpaper.

Required behaviors:

- age-aware defaults
- stricter interaction protections for younger users
- crisis, bullying, and exploitation escalation pathways
- removal of explicit contamination from youth-adjacent surfaces
- explainable safety messaging for guardians and educators where relevant

## User Experience Rules

### What DFC Should Feel Like

- disciplined
- premium
- safe
- calm under pressure
- collaborative
- mentor-led
- future-facing

### What DFC Must Not Feel Like

- trashy
- predatory
- defamation-heavy
- compulsive by design
- socially corrosive
- permissive toward poison because it drives metrics

## Metrics Standard

DFC should track growth and health together.

### Growth Metrics

- event conversion
- replay conversion
- qualified registrations
- retained communities
- partnership activation

### Health Metrics

- harassment rate
- report-to-resolution time
- repeat-offender containment rate
- community health score
- mentor/helpful contribution rate
- youth-safety incident rate
- appeal accuracy rate

## Release Gate Standard

No major communications, community, or immersive feature should ship without:

1. moderation pathway
2. reporting pathway
3. block or access-control pathway
4. youth-safety review
5. community-health measurement plan
6. rollback or containment plan

## Out Of Scope For Betaverse-Labeled Features

- addiction-first dark patterns
- humiliation-based engagement loops
- unmoderated immersive gathering spaces
- explicit-content tolerance in general surfaces
- fake neutrality toward clear social poisoning

## Working Test

Before shipping a feature, DFC should ask:

1. Does this strengthen people or just stimulate them?
2. Does this reduce poison or make poison easier to spread?
3. Does this support mentors, communities, and partners?
4. Would we be proud for a young person to learn digital behavior from this surface?

If the answer fails those tests, the feature is not Betaverse-ready.
