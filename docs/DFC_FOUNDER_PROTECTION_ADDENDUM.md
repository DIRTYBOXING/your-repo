# DataFightCentral Founder Protection Addendum

**Version 1.0 | Effective Date: March 10, 2026**

---

## Purpose

This Founder Protection Addendum ("Addendum") supplements the DataFightCentral Promoter Partnership Agreement ("Main Agreement") between DataFightCentral Pty Ltd ("DFC") and a Partner Promoter ("Promoter").

This Addendum applies only if both parties sign it. If there is a conflict between this Addendum and the Main Agreement, this Addendum controls for the subjects covered below.

This document is a business template and should be reviewed by licensed counsel before execution.

---

## 1. Non-Interference and Non-Circumvention

Promoter agrees that during the term of the Main Agreement and for 24 months after termination, Promoter will not, directly or indirectly:

- interfere with DFC's business relationships with sponsors, venues, broadcasters, payment providers, or contracted talent;
- bypass DFC to replicate DFC-negotiated commercial terms with DFC-introduced counterparties;
- induce any DFC partner or vendor to breach obligations owed to DFC;
- make false or misleading claims intended to damage DFC's contractual relationships.

Allowed conduct:

- ordinary fair competition that does not involve confidential information misuse, deception, or contract interference;
- independent business relationships not introduced by DFC and not sourced from DFC confidential data.

---

## 2. Non-Disparagement and Reputation Protection

Each party agrees not to publish knowingly false statements about the other party, its officers, staff, or affiliated events.

This clause does not restrict:

- truthful statements required by law or court order;
- good-faith reports to police, regulators, or legal counsel;
- evidence submitted in a legal proceeding.

Before public escalation, parties must first use the dispute process in the Main Agreement unless immediate legal protection is required.

---

## 3. Anti-Harassment and Safety

Promoter, DFC, and their representatives must not engage in harassment, intimidation, stalking, threats, coercion, or targeted abuse in connection with platform operations.

If a credible safety incident is reported:

- DFC may temporarily suspend campaign visibility, payouts, or account permissions while investigating;
- parties must preserve evidence (messages, emails, call logs, CCTV, social posts);
- the issue may be escalated to law enforcement where appropriate.

No party is required to tolerate unsafe conduct as a precondition to continuing business.

---

## 4. Talent, Staff, and Student Non-Poaching

During the Main Agreement and for 18 months after termination, neither party will actively solicit for hire or engagement the other party's core staff, contracted production personnel, or rostered athletes introduced through the partnership, unless:

- prior written consent is obtained; or
- the individual independently applies through a public posting without targeted solicitation.

General advertising that is not targeted is allowed.

---

## 5. Trademark, Brand, and Event Identity Protection

- Each party retains all right, title, and interest in its trademarks, trade names, logos, and event brands.
- No transfer of ownership occurs unless expressly stated in a separate written assignment.
- Promoter may not register, attempt to register, or challenge marks confusingly similar to DFC marks.
- DFC may not register, attempt to register, or challenge marks confusingly similar to Promoter marks.
- On termination, each party must stop unauthorized use of the other's marks within 7 days, except archived factual references permitted by law.

---

## 6. Confidential Information and Operational Data

Confidential Information includes business plans, pricing models, payout reports, customer lists, lead lists, contracts, and private operational processes.

Each party must:

- use Confidential Information only to perform the Main Agreement;
- protect it with reasonable security controls;
- not disclose it except to legal/accounting advisors under confidentiality obligations.

Confidentiality survives for 5 years after termination, except trade secrets survive as long as protected under law.

---

## 7. Evidence, Audit Trail, and Incident Logging

To reduce factual disputes, both parties agree to maintain and preserve:

- contract versions and signed amendments;
- payout records and transaction exports;
- communications regarding disputes, takedowns, and account actions;
- moderation/safety incident logs.

Upon written request in a dispute, each party must provide relevant non-privileged records within 10 business days.

---

## 8. Injunctive Relief

The parties acknowledge that breaches of Sections 1-6 may cause irreparable harm not fully compensable by monetary damages.

Either party may seek immediate injunctive or equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent ongoing harm, without waiving arbitration rights for final merits resolution.

---

---

## 10. Survival

Sections 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8 survive termination of the Main Agreement for the durations stated, or if unstated, for 24 months.

---

## 11. Governing Law and Disputes

Unless modified here, governing law and dispute resolution remain as defined in the Main Agreement.
