# DFC Global Distribution Command

Status: operating brief for taking DFC from Australia-first into multi-market distribution at platform level.

## Core Position

DFC should not present itself as an Australian app with some overseas links.

DFC should present itself as:

- the distribution control plane for combat sports
- the market router between promotions, viewers, gyms, creators, and media partners
- the rights-aware hub that can sell, promote, route, replay, and localize by region

That means YouTube is only one outlet. AU and NZ are only the home base. They are not the ceiling.

## Highest-Level Distribution Model

DFC needs four layers running together.

### 1. Owned Distribution

- DFC app
- DFC PPV
- DFC replay library
- DFC event pages
- DFC CRM, email, and push

This is where margin, data ownership, retargeting, and replay value stay strongest.

### 2. Partner Broadcast Distribution

- DAZN
- ESPN+
- UFC Fight Pass
- TrillerTV+
- TNT Sports
- Sky Sports
- Sony LIV
- JioCinema
- FanCode
- PTV Sports
- Tapmad
- Abu Dhabi Sports
- beIN Sports
- Shahid
- ABEMA
- U-NEXT
- SuperSport
- Combate Global

This is where DFC stops looking regional and starts looking like a routing and packaging company.

### 3. Activation Distribution

- fighters
- gyms
- coaches
- creators
- promoters
- sponsors
- local media

Every event needs role-specific packs, referral links, and regional variants.

### 4. Market Localization

- regional pricing
- local platform routing
- local creator packs
- local language captions
- local press targets
- time-zone specific launch windows

Without localization, “international” is just export, not expansion.

## Priority Market Ladder

### Tier 1: Immediate commercial expansion

- United States
- Canada
- United Kingdom
- Europe core markets
- MENA core markets

Reason:

- strongest rights, sponsorship, and premium PPV upside
- highest credibility gain for promoters
- best bridge from AU-rooted combat content into global legitimacy

### Tier 2: High-growth distribution lanes

- India
- Pakistan
- Southeast Asia
- Japan
- Korea
- Latin America

Reason:

- huge combat audiences
- strong mobile consumption
- better upside for creator-led activation and regional price ladders

### Tier 3: expansion moat

- Africa
- Greater China
- secondary European markets

Reason:

- long-term brand and rights positioning
- underbuilt combat discovery infrastructure

## What DFC Must Actually Show In Product

If DFC wants to feel international, the app must visibly communicate:

1. viewer-region routing, not just event-origin routing
2. market-specific platform options
3. regional pricing intent
4. replay and rights language that feels broadcast-grade
5. activation packs for gyms, fighters, creators, and sponsors

## Non-Negotiable Product Surfaces

### Event page

- show commercial state
- show replay policy
- show partner and direct distribution clearly
- show region-aware watch options

### Watch page

- feel like a premium broadcast destination
- show whether playback is direct, partner-routed, or rights-protected
- show replay continuity

### Library

- read like owned media inventory
- not just old purchases

### Promoter control room

- show where an event is being distributed
- show which markets are armed
- show whether launch packs and referral kits are issued

## Operator Command Sequence

1. build event identity and commerce surface
2. arm direct DFC route
3. arm partner distribution routes by market
4. issue activation packs to fighters, gyms, creators, and sponsors
5. localize pricing, captions, and creator payloads by priority market
6. monitor replay, rights, and settlement from one control surface

## Current Truth

DFC already has the beginnings of global routing in code.

What is missing is not just platform names.

What is missing is a product and operating posture that makes DFC look like the command center for international combat sports distribution.
