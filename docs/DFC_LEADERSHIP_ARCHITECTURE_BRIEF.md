# DFC Leadership Architecture Brief

## Position

Data Fight Central is not built to replace promotions, broadcasters, or gyms. It is built to amplify them.

DFC is the promoter of promoters: a combat sports operating system that helps events get discovered, merchandised, sold, streamed, replayed, and measured across every level of the market, from regional shows to global cards.

## Leadership Mandate

The leadership model should align Development, Marketing, and Research around one shared operating object: the canonical event.

Every team should be asking the same question:

How does DFC help this event get more visibility, stronger conversion, cleaner distribution, and longer replay value?

## The Three Leadership Pillars

### 1. Development

Development owns the product control plane.

Responsibilities:

- canonical event identity and data quality
- artwork resolution, media approvals, and rendering reliability
- pricing, checkout, entitlement, and replay flows
- operator tooling for promoters and internal staff
- event pages, PPV surfaces, maps, feed injection, and partner APIs
- observability, security, performance, and uptime

Development success means DFC can turn raw event metadata into a production-grade commercial surface quickly and safely.

### 2. Marketing

Marketing owns amplification and conversion.

Responsibilities:

- event launch plans and campaign packaging
- promoter-facing growth playbooks
- cross-channel distribution from the canonical event page
- regional pricing and promotional framing
- sponsor inventory presentation
- owned, earned, and partner channel syndication

Marketing success means every event gets promoted like it matters, not buried as a passive listing.

### 3. Research

Research owns signal, intelligence, and market adaptation.

Responsibilities:

- partner landscape tracking
- promoter pain-point mapping
- audience behavior and pricing sensitivity
- region-by-region demand analysis
- source trust scoring and metadata quality auditing
- product benchmarking against platforms such as Paramount+, Kayo, Foxtel, and Main Event

Research success means DFC makes evidence-based decisions about what to promote, how to package it, and where to expand next.

## Shared Operating Model

The three pillars should run on one loop:

1. Source event data and official promotion signals.
2. Normalize into the canonical event graph.
3. Resolve artwork, links, pricing, and distribution metadata.
4. Publish canonical event pages and event commerce surfaces.
5. Syndicate into feed, social, search, maps, partner channels, and replay.
6. Measure CTR, conversion, entitlement, retention, and replay performance.
7. Feed those learnings back into product, campaigns, and research priorities.

## Platform Control Points

DFC should own the control points that matter commercially.

- canonical event page
- official poster and banner resolution
- ticket and PPV destination routing
- live watch entry and replay entry
- regional offer presentation
- promoter analytics and campaign reporting
- sponsor inventory and brand-safe placement

These are the areas where DFC creates leverage for both large and small promotions.

## Why This Matters

Most platforms force smaller promotions to look unfinished. DFC should do the opposite.

- If a promotion has a real poster, DFC should resolve and showcase it.
- If a promotion has a real ticket or PPV destination, DFC should route people there cleanly.
- If a promotion is regional, DFC should still package it with the same seriousness as a top-tier event.

That is how DFC becomes the global promotion layer instead of just another combat content app.

## Strategic Benchmarking

### Paramount+

Strong at warehousing rights and distributing premium content, but not designed as a promoter growth operating system.

### Kayo

Strong at sports merchandising and event presentation. DFC should match the quality of event packaging while staying promoter-first.

### Foxtel / Main Event

Strong at premium PPV packaging, pricing, and entitlement control. DFC should bring that discipline to a broader promoter network, including smaller shows.

### DFC Advantage

DFC can unify discovery, promotion, commerce, and replay for the full combat sports ecosystem instead of operating as a closed broadcaster silo.

## 2026 Execution Priorities

1. Make every important event resolve to a strong canonical page with real art and real links.
2. Build promoter-facing merchandising and campaign tooling on top of the canonical event graph.
3. Expand region-aware PPV, replay, and sponsorship packaging.
4. Use research to prioritize under-served promotions and markets where DFC can create immediate lift.
5. Keep the doctrine explicit: DFC promotes promotions.

## Operating Standard

No toy surfaces. No fake event treatment. No generic corporate flattening.

The standard is simple: if an event is worth selling, it is worth packaging like a real event.
