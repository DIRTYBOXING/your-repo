## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

You need three videos for the Google Grant application:

1. **Walkthrough Video** (3-4 min): Product demo showing features
2. **Testimonial Video(s)** (1-2 min each): User/fighter stories

## PART 2: RECORDING STEP-BY-STEP

### WALKTHROUGH VIDEO (3-4 minutes)

#### Setup (30 minutes)

1. **Choose your device:** Mac (QuickTime ✓) or PC (OBS Studio ✓ Free)
2. **Install recording software:**
   - **Mac:** Built-in QuickTime (Command + Shift + 5)
   - **PC:** [Download OBS Studio](https://obsproject.com/) (Free)
   - **Alternative:** ScreenFlow (Mac, $99) or Camtasia (All platforms, $130)

3. **Test recording:**
   - Record 30 seconds of the app
   - Check audio levels (voiceover should be louder than background noise)
   - Check resolution (1080p minimum)

#### Recording the Walkthrough (1-2 hours)

1. **Open the app** (use demo/staging environment if available)
2. **Record each section:**
   - Dashboard (go slow, tap buttons deliberately)
   - Mentor profiles (highlight verification badges)
   - Crisis resources (emphasize clarity and instantly accessible)
   - Community feed (show diverse voices)
3. **Re-do takes if needed** (natural mistakes are fine, skip if unclear)
4. **Total raw footage:** 5-7 minutes (will trim in editing)

#### Audio Recording (30 minutes, can be done later)

1. **Find a quiet room** (bedroom, closet, interior room with soft furnishings absorb sound)
2. **Plug in USB microphone** or use smartphone earbuds with mic
3. **Open audio recording app:**
   - Mac: Voice Memos app
   - Windows: Audacity (Free)
   - All: Voice Memos app on phone
4. **Read from script** (walk through at natural pace, 3:20 total)
5. **Record 1-2 backup takes** (use the best one)
6. **Export as:** MP3 or WAV file

---

### TESTIMONIAL VIDEO (1-2 minutes each)

#### Pre-Interview (1 hour before)

1. **Contact fighter/mentor** ("Can you do a quick interview for 15 min?")
2. **Find location:** Well-lit room (natural window light is ideal)
3. **Set up camera:** Tripod, smartphone or webcam, 3-4 feet away
4. **Do audio check:** Have subject say a few words, check volume
5. **Warm up:** Chat for 5 min before rolling (puts them at ease)

#### Interview Recording (15-20 minutes)

1. **Start rolling** (record continuously, don't stop between questions)
2. **Ask open-ended questions:**
   - "Tell me what was going on before you found DataFightCentral"
   - "What changed after you started using the app?"
   - "What would you say to other fighters considering it?"
3. **Be silent after they answer** (they often go deeper)
4. **Get 2-3 takes** if possible (people loosen up after first take)
5. **End with:** "Thanks, that was great" (genuine closing feels good)

#### Post-Interview (10 minutes)

1. **Get written consent** (use phone to take a photo of signed form)
2. **Ask about anonymity:** "Do you want your real name on this?"
3. **Get their contact:** "We'll send you the final video before we post it"

## PART 3: EDITING (Using Free Software)

### Install DaVinci Resolve (Free Version)

1. **Download:** [davinci resolve.com](https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve/)
2. **Install:** Takes 5 minutes
3. **YouTube tutorial:** Search "DaVinci Resolve basics" (20 min tutorial)

### Editing Workflow (2-3 hours per video)

#### Step 1: Import Files

1. Create new project (1920×1080, 24fps)
2. Import video files (walkthrough recording OR testimonial)
3. Import audio file (voiceover for walkthrough)
4. Import music file (royalty-free, see music section below)

#### Step 2: Basic Editing

1. **Cut the timeline:** Remove long pauses, bad takes
2. **Sync audio:** Line up voiceover with screen recording
3. **Add fade-in/fade-out** at start and end (0.5 seconds each)
4. **Trim to length:**
   - Walkthrough: 3:20-3:50
   - Testimonial: 1:00-2:00

#### Step 3: Color & Effects (Optional)

1. **Color grade:** Very light adjustment (boost contrast +5%, saturation +10%)
2. **Add transitions:** Fade between sections (0.3 seconds)
3. **Title cards:** (optional) "DataFightCentral" at start, website at end

#### Step 4: Audio Mix

1. **Voiceover level:** -3dB (so music is quieter)
2. **Background music:** -12dB (not overpowering)
3. **Listen with headphones** to check balance

#### Step 5: Export

1. **Settings:**
   - Format: H.264
   - Resolution: 1080p
   - Bitrate: 8-12 Mbps
   - Frame rate: 24p
2. **File naming:** `DataFightCentral_Walkthrough_FINAL.mp4`
3. **Estimated file size:** 300-600 MB
4. **Export time:** 5-10 minutes

## PART 5: UPLOAD & SUBMISSION

### YouTube Upload

1. **Create new video:**
   - Go to [youtube.com > Upload](youtube.com/upload)
   - Choose file (drag and drop or browse)
   - Title: "DataFightCentral - [Video Type]"
   - Description: Include link to website, crisis resources
   - Tags: "mental health", "fighter", "app", "crisis support"
   - Visibility: **UNLISTED** (only people with link can view)

2. **Add captions:**
   - YouTube auto-generates (usually 80% accurate)
   - Review and fix [manually](youtube.com/editing) for accessibility
   - Download SRT file if needed

3. **Wait for processing:** 5-30 minutes depending on video length

### Submission Checklist

- [ ] Walkthrough video (3-4 min) ✓ Uploaded to YouTube (Unlisted)
- [ ] Testimonial video(s) (1-2 min each) ✓ Uploaded to YouTube (Unlisted)
- [ ] All videos have captions
- [ ] Video URLs copied and ready
- [ ] Video descriptions include:
  - Website URL
  - Crisis hotline number (if applicable)
  - Contact email
- [ ] Google Application form ready for submission

## PART 6: QUICK TROUBLESHOOTING

| Problem                       | Solution                                                                      |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Audio too quiet**           | Re-record voiceover closer to microphone, or boost in editing (-3dB → 0dB)    |
| **Video is pixelated**        | Record at 1080p or higher; export at 8Mbps bitrate minimum                    |
| **Background noise**          | Record in bedroom/closet; use lavalier mic; record during quiet time          |
| **Subject is nervous**        | Chat for 10 min before filming; do multiple takes; focus on second/third take |
| **Editing is slow**           | Close other apps; use lower resolution preview during editing                 |
| **YouTube won't accept file** | Check format (H.264), try MP4 wrapper, file size under 128GB                  |

## PART 7: OPTIONAL ENHANCEMENTS

### If You Want to Level Up:

**Add B-Roll Footage** (optional):

- Film actual screeen-recording app walkthrough on phone/tablet (different angles)
- Film fighters training (if available)
- Film people using the app in real-time
- Cut between these during voiceover (adds visual interest)

**Animated Graphics:**

- Use Canva Pro ($120/year) to add subtle animations
- Stats appear on screen as voiceover mentions them
- Example: "36% of fighters report depression" → [Number animates in]

**Professional Voiceover:**

- Post on Fiverr ($50-200 for professional voice actor)
- Or use text-to-speech tool (natural-sounding: Google Play/Azure Speech)

## SUBMISSION TO GOOGLE GRANTS

When you're ready to apply for Google Ad Grants or Google for Nonprofits, the form will ask:

- **"Demo URL:"** [Paste unlisted walkthrough video YouTube link]
- **"Company/Organization video:"** [Paste testimonial YouTube link]
- **"Featured media:"** [Can attach any of the above]

Google wants to see:

1. ✅ Product use case is clear
2. ✅ User testimonials are authentic
3. ✅ Mission/impact is compelling
4. ✅ Technical quality is professional (not polished, just clear)

You're set!
