# DFC Partner MOU Template

## Memorandum of Understanding

This Memorandum of Understanding is entered into by and between:

- Party A: Data Fight Central Pty Ltd and its social impact program arm (DFC)
- Party B: [Partner Organization Name]

Effective date: [Date]

## 1. Purpose
This MOU sets out the collaboration framework for delivery of the selected DFC campaign in [City or Region], including Pink Shields, Buy a Coffee Not a Coffin, and Gold Coin Gym.

## 2. Scope of collaboration
Both parties agree to collaborate on one or more of the following:
- survivor support and referral pathways
- community workshops and prevention activity
- mentorship and youth pathways
- fundraising and campaign delivery
- impact measurement and reporting

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

### DFC responsibilities
- Program coordination and campaign management
- Donation processing and campaign reporting infrastructure
- Partner communication and review cadence
- Campaign media and outreach support
- Disbursement controls according to agreed criteria

### Partner responsibilities
- Local delivery capability for agreed services
- Beneficiary verification and local case coordination
- Compliance with safeguarding and privacy requirements
- Accurate reporting of agreed impact metrics
- Prompt escalation of safety or legal issues

## 4. Safeguarding and child protection
Partner confirms it has and will maintain:
- safeguarding policy and mandatory reporting process
- background-check process for all eligible roles
- incident escalation and response process
- documented child-protection training records where required

## 5. Data and privacy
- Parties agree to collect and process only necessary personal data.
- Sensitive data must be protected using reasonable technical and organizational safeguards.
- Public reporting should avoid personally identifying survivors or minors.
- Survivor names and identifying details require explicit informed consent before publication.

## 6. Funds and accounting
- Campaign-linked funds follow documented allocation and reconciliation procedures.
- DFC will maintain designated fund routing for campaign receipts.
- Partner will provide usage records and beneficiary documentation required for reconciliation.
- Both parties maintain records suitable for audit, donor review, and statutory reporting where applicable.

## 7. Branding and communications
- Use of DFC and partner names, marks, or logos requires prior written approval.
- Survivor stories or testimonials require explicit informed consent.
- Neither party may make misleading public claims regarding impact outcomes.
- Joint press releases require mutual written sign-off.

## 8. Term and termination
- Initial term: [12 months] from signature date unless otherwise agreed.
- Review cadence: monthly operational review and quarterly strategic review.
- Either party may terminate with [30] days written notice.
- Immediate suspension is permitted for safeguarding, legal, or trust breaches.
- Funds already committed to approved beneficiaries remain ring-fenced for delivery.

## 9. Liability, insurance, and indemnity
- Each party is responsible for its own acts, omissions, and compliance obligations.
- Partner will maintain suitable public liability and program insurance where legally required.
- Each party indemnifies the other for losses arising from its material breach or negligence.

## 10. Confidentiality
- Parties will keep confidential beneficiary information and sensitive operational details.

## 11. Dispute resolution
- Parties will attempt good-faith resolution first, then mediation in [jurisdiction] if unresolved.

## 12. Legal status
- This MOU documents collaboration intent and operating expectations.
- Except where separately contracted in writing, this document does not create an exclusive relationship.
