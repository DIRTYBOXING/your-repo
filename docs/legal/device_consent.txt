DEVICE CONSENT AND DATA COLLECTION AGREEMENT
DataFight Central — Safety Zone System

Last updated: 2026-03-27

1. INTRODUCTION
This document outlines the data collected by the DataFight Central (DFC)
safety zone system through wearable devices and geofence monitoring.

2. DATA COLLECTED
The following data points are collected from registered safety devices:
  - Device identifier (hardware serial number)
  - GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude)
  - Accelerometer readings (impact force, orientation)
  - Proximity sensor readings (distance to nearby devices)
  - Battery level
  - Panic button activation status
  - Timestamp of each reading

3. PURPOSE OF DATA COLLECTION
Data is collected solely for:
  - Fighter and participant safety during events
  - Geofence zone enforcement (restricted areas)
  - Emergency alert dispatch (SOS, fall detection)
  - Post-event safety audit and incident investigation

4. DATA RETENTION
  - Real-time alert data: retained for 90 days
  - Incident logs (SOS, fall, geofence breach): retained for 2 years
  - Heartbeat/routine data: retained for 30 days
  - All data deleted upon written request (subject to legal holds)

5. DATA SHARING
Data is shared only with:
  - On-call safety team (SMS/email alerts)
  - Event medical personnel (during active incidents)
  - Law enforcement (only upon valid legal process)
Data is never sold or shared with advertisers.

6. CONSENT
By wearing a DFC safety device at a registered event, the participant
acknowledges and consents to the data collection described above.

Participants may withdraw consent at any time by:
  - Removing the device and returning it to event staff
  - Sending a written request to legal@datafightcentral.com

7. SECURITY
All device data is transmitted over encrypted channels (TLS 1.3).
Alert payloads are validated with HMAC-SHA256 signatures.
Access to safety logs is restricted to authorized personnel.
