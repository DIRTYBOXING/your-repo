# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.1  "IP" means all intellectual property including but not limited to:
     (a) Source code (Flutter/Dart, TypeScript, Python, Node.js, Zephyr RTOS)
     (b) Algorithms (fight matching, feed ranking, quantum optimization,
         combat intelligence, AI swarm coordination, detection/triangulation)
     (c) AI Systems (78 autonomous agents, 25 engines, Samurai Swarm,
         DFC Nexus, Atlas multi-model router)
     (d) Cryptographic protocols (device verification, chain of custody,
         HMAC-SHA256 evidence signing, nonce replay protection)
     (e) Hardware firmware (nRF52840 Zephyr RTOS, BLE/UWB protocols)
     (f) Drone control systems (fleet management, detection, mission planning)
     (g) Data models (37+ models), database schemas (47+ collections)
     (h) Trade secrets (business logic, pricing, matching weights)
     (i) Trademarks ("Data Fight Central", "DFC", "CHUCKYA", "Blackbird",
         "Samurai Swarm", "Atlas", all logos and design marks)
     (j) Copyrights (all original works of authorship)
     (k) Patents and patent applications (current and future)
     (l) Domain names, social media accounts, digital identities
     (m) Documentation, technical specifications, API designs
     (n) Training data, model weights, AI configurations
     (o) UX/UI designs, visual identity, neon theme system

1.2  "Family" means the Founder and all direct bloodline descendants
     (children, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, etc.) of the Founder,
     whether biological or legally adopted.

1.3  "IP Trust" means the [Family Name] Intellectual Property Dynasty Trust,
     the legal entity that holds all IP described in Section 1.1.

1.4  "OpCo" means Data Fight Central LLC (or successor operating entity),
     the business that operates the platform under license from the IP Trust.

1.5  "Founder" means the original creator and developer of Data Fight Central.

1.6  "Trustee" means the person(s) authorized to make decisions for the
     IP Trust. Initially the Founder; succession per Article 5.

1.7  "Investor" means any person or entity that acquires equity, debt,
     or revenue participation rights in the OpCo ONLY.

1.8  "IP License" means the exclusive, revocable license granted by the
     IP Trust to the OpCo (see Article 4).


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 2 — CORE PRINCIPLES (IRREVOCABLE)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1  BLOODLINE LOCK: The IP shall NEVER be owned, assigned, transferred,
     sold, gifted, pledged, encumbered, or conveyed to any person or
     entity outside the Family, except as expressly permitted by the
     Founder under Article 6 (Investor Participation).

2.2  SEPARATION OF IP AND BUSINESS: The IP Trust and the OpCo are and
     shall remain SEPARATE legal entities at all times. The IP is NOT
     an asset of the OpCo. Investors in the OpCo acquire NO rights,
     title, or interest in the IP.

2.3  FAMILY SOVEREIGNTY: No court order, bankruptcy proceeding, divorce
     settlement, creditor claim, or corporate action of the OpCo shall
     have any power to pierce, transfer, encumber, or extinguish the
     Family's ownership of the IP. The IP Trust is structured to be
     judgment-proof and creditor-protected.

2.4  ANTI-DILUTION: No action by any Family member, Trustee, officer,
     director, employee, contractor, or agent shall dilute or diminish
     the Family's 100% ownership of the IP without unanimous written
     consent of all living Family members over age 18.

2.5  NO OPEN SOURCE: Notwithstanding any prior LICENSE file (including
     the historical MIT License in this repository), ALL IP is
     proprietary and confidential. The MIT License is hereby REVOKED
     for all code authored after the Effective Date of this Charter.
     Pre-existing open-source dependencies (third-party libraries) are
     excluded from this restriction.


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 3 — IP TRUST STRUCTURE
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1  FORMATION: The Founder shall establish:
     (a) [Family Name] IP Dynasty Trust (irrevocable, spendthrift trust)
     (b) Jurisdiction: [State/Country — e.g., Nevada, South Dakota, or
         Wyoming for strongest trust protection in the US]
     (c) Duration: Perpetual (or maximum allowed by jurisdiction)

3.2  TRUST ASSETS: Upon formation, the Founder assigns ALL IP (as defined
     in Section 1.1) to the Trust. This includes:
     - All existing source code in this repository
     - All GitHub repositories under DIRTYBOXING
     - All domain names (datafightcentral.com and variations)
     - All trademarks (filed and common law)
     - All trade secrets and proprietary algorithms
     - All AI systems, models, and training configurations
     - All firmware and hardware designs
     - All future IP created for or used by the DFC platform

3.3  TRUSTEE APPOINTMENT:
     (a) Initial Trustee: The Founder
     (b) Successor Trustee: Per Article 5 (Succession)
     (c) Institutional Co-Trustee (recommended): A licensed trust company
         to ensure continuity if no Family member is available
     (d) Trust Protector: An independent attorney or firm appointed by
         the Founder to oversee Trustee compliance with this Charter

3.4  BENEFICIARIES:
     (a) Primary: The Founder
     (b) Secondary: Founder's children (equal shares)
     (c) Tertiary: Founder's grandchildren (per stirpes)
     (d) Perpetual: All direct bloodline descendants, in perpetuity
     (e) EXCLUDED: Spouses, in-laws, step-relatives, friends, business
         partners, investors, employees, contractors, and all other
         persons not of direct bloodline descent

3.5  SPENDTHRIFT PROTECTION: No beneficiary may assign, alienate,
     pledge, or encumber their beneficial interest. Creditors of any
     beneficiary have NO claim against the Trust assets.


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 4 — IP LICENSE TO OPERATING COMPANY
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1  GRANT: The IP Trust grants the OpCo an exclusive, revocable license
     to use the IP solely for operating the Data Fight Central platform
     and related services.

4.2  LICENSE FEE: The OpCo shall pay the IP Trust a royalty of:
     (a) [X]% of gross revenue (recommended: 10-20%), OR
     (b) A fixed annual fee of $[amount], whichever is greater
     (c) Paid quarterly within 30 days of quarter-end
     (d) This fee is a legitimate business expense of the OpCo and
         reduces OpCo taxable income

4.3  REVOCATION TRIGGERS: The Trustee may revoke the license if:
     (a) OpCo fails to pay license fees for two consecutive quarters
     (b) OpCo attempts to assign, sublicense, or transfer the IP
     (c) OpCo enters bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings
     (d) OpCo is acquired by a third party without Trustee consent
     (e) OpCo materially breaches any term of the license agreement
     (f) The Founder (or successor Trustee) determines revocation is
         in the best interest of the Family

4.4  EFFECT OF REVOCATION: Upon revocation, the OpCo must immediately
     cease all use of the IP, return all copies, and delete all
     digital copies. Investors in the OpCo are left with equity in a
     company that has no IP — this is the structural firewall.

4.5  SUB-LICENSING: The OpCo may NOT sub-license the IP to any third
     party without prior written consent of the Trustee.

4.6  IMPROVEMENTS: Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works
     created by the OpCo, its employees, or contractors using the IP
     are automatically assigned to and owned by the IP Trust.


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 5 — SUCCESSION RULES
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1  TRUSTEE SUCCESSION ORDER:
     (a) First: Founder's eldest child who is willing and able
     (b) Second: Next eldest child willing and able
     (c) Third: Founder's eldest grandchild willing and able
     (d) Fourth: Professional trust company (institutional trustee)
     (e) The Trust Protector resolves disputes about succession

5.2  REQUIREMENTS FOR SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE:
     (a) Must be a direct bloodline descendant of the Founder
     (b) Must be at least 21 years of age
     (c) Must not be under any legal incapacity
     (d) Must agree in writing to be bound by this Charter
     (e) Should have technical understanding of the IP (preferred)

5.3  INCAPACITY OF TRUSTEE: If the current Trustee becomes incapacitated:
     (a) The Trust Protector shall appoint the next eligible successor
     (b) If no eligible successor, the institutional co-trustee takes over
     (c) Decisions during interim are limited to maintenance only

5.4  EDUCATION MANDATE: The Trustee shall make reasonable efforts to
     educate the next generation of Family members about:
     (a) The nature and value of the IP
     (b) The technology stack and its capabilities
     (c) The governance structure and their rights/obligations
     (d) Basic cybersecurity and IP protection practices

5.5  DEAD HAND PROVISION: Even if all current beneficiaries disclaim
     interest, the IP remains in Trust for future descendants. The IP
     may NEVER be abandoned, dedicated to the public domain, or
     released under an open-source license without unanimous consent
     of all living Family members over age 18 AND the Trust Protector.


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 6 — INVESTOR PARTICIPATION MODEL
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.1  STRUCTURE: Investors may ONLY acquire:
     (a) Equity in the OpCo (the business), OR
     (b) Revenue participation rights in the OpCo, OR
     (c) Convertible notes or SAFEs against OpCo equity
     Investors may NEVER acquire any ownership, license, lien,
     security interest, or encumbrance on the IP itself.

6.2  WHAT INVESTORS GET:
     (a) Percentage ownership of the OpCo (negotiated per round)
     (b) Rights to OpCo dividends and distributions
     (c) OpCo board seats (if negotiated — Founder retains majority)
     (d) Information rights (OpCo financials, not IP details)
     (e) Pro rata participation in future OpCo rounds
     (f) Liquidation preference on OpCo assets (NOT the IP)

6.3  WHAT INVESTORS DO NOT GET:
     (a) Any ownership of the IP
     (b) Any license to use the IP independently
     (c) Any security interest or lien on the IP
     (d) Any right to force-sell, encumber, or transfer the IP
     (e) Any access to source code, algorithms, or trade secrets
         beyond what is necessary for due diligence (under NDA)
     (f) Any right to prevent the Trustee from revoking the OpCo license
     (g) Any claim on the IP if the OpCo is dissolved or bankrupt

6.4  INVESTOR ACKNOWLEDGMENT: Every investor must sign a statement:

     "I acknowledge that all intellectual property used by [OpCo Name]
      is owned by [Family Name] IP Dynasty Trust and is licensed to
      [OpCo Name] under a revocable license. My investment is in the
      operating company ONLY. I have no rights, claims, or interests
      in the intellectual property, and I understand that the license
      may be revoked under the terms of the IP License Agreement."

6.5  VALUATION: The IP License is a line item in OpCo's expenses. When
     investors value the OpCo, they value the business INCLUSIVE of the
     license cost. The IP itself is valued separately on the Trust's
     books and is NOT part of the OpCo's balance sheet.

6.6  IP VALUATION PARTICIPATION: If the Founder chooses to allow an
     investor to participate in the VALUE of the IP (without ownership):
     (a) This is done via a separate "IP Revenue Participation Agreement"
     (b) The investor receives a percentage of the license fees paid
         by the OpCo to the IP Trust
     (c) This is a contractual right, NOT an ownership interest
     (d) The participation is time-limited (e.g., 10 years) and
         terminates automatically
     (e) The Founder retains sole discretion to offer this


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 7 — TECHNICAL ENFORCEMENT
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

7.1  CODE HEADERS: All source files shall include the IP notice header
     (see legal/IP_HEADER.txt)

7.2  REPOSITORY ACCESS: The GitHub repository shall be PRIVATE. Access
     is granted only to:
     (a) Family members who are beneficiaries of the Trust
     (b) Employees/contractors under signed work-for-hire + NDA
     (c) The Trust Protector (read-only, for compliance verification)

7.3  CONTRIBUTOR AGREEMENTS: All non-Family contributors must sign:
     (a) Contributor License Agreement assigning all work to the Trust
     (b) Non-Disclosure Agreement (survives termination indefinitely)
     (c) Non-Compete (where legally enforceable, 24 months)

7.4  IP REGISTRY: A machine-readable registry of all sovereign IP
     components shall be maintained at legal/IP_REGISTRY.json

7.5  AUDIT TRAIL: All commits, deployments, and infrastructure changes
     are logged and attributable to prevent unauthorized IP extraction.


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 8 — PROHIBITED ACTIONS
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8.1  No person (including the Founder) may:
     (a) Sell, assign, or transfer the IP outside the Family
     (b) Grant any security interest or lien on the IP
     (c) Release the IP under any open-source license
     (d) Allow the IP to enter the public domain
     (e) Use the IP to secure any loan or debt
     (f) Merge the IP Trust with the OpCo
     (g) Allow any court to order disposition of the IP
     (h) Create any obligation that could result in IP forfeiture

8.2  EXCEPTION: The Founder (and ONLY the Founder during their lifetime)
     may authorize limited, time-bound, non-exclusive licenses of
     specific IP components to third parties, provided:
     (a) The license is in writing
     (b) The license has a fixed term (max 5 years, renewable)
     (c) The license fee is at fair market value
     (d) The licensee signs the Investor Acknowledgment (Section 6.4)
     (e) The license does not diminish Family ownership


# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ARTICLE 9 — EMERGENCY PROVISIONS
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

9.1  If the OpCo is subject to hostile acquisition:
     (a) The Trustee shall immediately revoke the IP License
     (b) The IP Trust may license the IP to a NEW OpCo formed by Family
     (c) Investors in the old OpCo retain old OpCo equity (now worthless)

9.2  If a Family member attempts to sell their beneficial interest:
     (a) The interest is void — spendthrift clause prevents transfer
     (b) The Trust Protector shall remove that member from Trustee
         eligibility for 10 years

9.3  If a creditor attempts to reach Trust assets:
     (a) The spendthrift clause protects against creditor claims
     (b) The Trustee shall engage legal counsel immediately
     (c) The Trust Protector has authority to move the Trust to a
         more protective jurisdiction if necessary
