# 2. MANDATORY IP ACKNOWLEDGMENT
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1  The Investor hereby acknowledges, understands, and agrees:

     (a) ALL intellectual property used by the OpCo — including but
         not limited to source code, algorithms, AI systems, firmware,
         trademarks, trade secrets, patents, and all proprietary
         technology — is owned by [Family Name] IP Dynasty Trust
         (the "IP Trust"), a separate legal entity.

     (b) The OpCo operates under an EXCLUSIVE BUT REVOCABLE license
         from the IP Trust. This license may be terminated under the
         conditions specified in the IP License Agreement.

     (c) The Investor's investment is in the OPERATING COMPANY ONLY.
         The Investor acquires NO rights, title, interest, claim,
         lien, security interest, or encumbrance of any kind in the
         intellectual property.

     (d) In the event the IP license is revoked, the OpCo may become
         unable to operate the platform. The Investor accepts this
         risk as part of the investment.

     (e) The IP Trust is controlled by the Founder's family and is
         NOT subject to the governance, voting, or control of OpCo
         shareholders or directors.

     (f) The Investor shall NOT:
         - Attempt to acquire the IP through any means
         - Claim any interest in the IP in any proceeding
         - Support any action that would transfer the IP from the Trust
         - Use voting rights to force an IP transfer or encumbrance
         - Seek to merge the OpCo and the IP Trust

     (g) This acknowledgment is a MATERIAL TERM of the investment.
         Breach of this Section is grounds for immediate forfeiture
         of Investor's equity at the lesser of cost or fair market value.

     - Investor receives pro rata share of sale proceeds
     - IP remains with the Trust
     - New owner must negotiate a new IP license with the Trust
     - No guarantee the Trust will license to the acquirer

6.2  OPCO IPO:
     - Investor's shares become publicly tradeable
     - IP remains with the Trust (disclosed as a risk factor)
     - License terms are disclosed in SEC filings

6.3  OPCO DISSOLUTION:
     - Investor receives pro rata share of remaining OpCo assets
     - IP returns to the Trust (it never left)
     - Investor has NO claim on the IP
